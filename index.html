Body Mass Index

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a numerical value derived from a person's weight and height to assess body fat levels. It helps classify individuals into categories such as underweight, normal weight, overweight, and obese. BMI serves as a simple screening tool to identify potential health risks associated with weight. Maintaining a healthy BMI can lower the chances of developing conditions like heart disease, diabetes, and hypertension. However, BMI does not distinguish between muscle and fat, making it less precise for certain individuals.
